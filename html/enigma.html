Here I challenge you to solve some interesting math and physics related problems that I have encountered, or thought of throughtout years. Note that while selecting the problems, I have focused more on how interesting the problem is and how enjoyable the solution process is, rather than the hardness of them.

Problems

  1. Find the surface area and the volume of an n-dimensional sphere.

  2. How many degrees of freedom does an n-sided polygon have?

  3. Imagine two identical objects with heat capacity \(C\) and temperatures \(T_1\) and \(T_2\). If we were to build an engine out of them, what is the maximum work that this engine can do? \(^1\)

  4. Give the shape of a fixed-volume planet (in mathematical form) that maximizes the gravitational force acting on a person standing on it.

  5. A ballistic missile is launched from the north pole, the target is at the latitude \(\phi\). To have the minimal launch velocity of the missile, at which angle (with respect to the horizon) do we need to launch it? \(^2\)

  6. Imagine a homogenous, liquid planet of average radius \(R \) and density \(\rho\), spinning around itself with an angular velocity of \(w.\) Find its eccentricity.

  7. A point particle of mass \(m\) and charge \(q\) is moving under a homogenous magnetic field of induction \(B\) and electrical field of intensity E, directed perpendicular to each other. Ignoring gravity and relativity, find its trajectory.\(^1\)

  8. Imagine a water sprinkler in the garden, sprinkling water uniformly at each angle and in all directions at the same time. What radius from the origin would get watered the most?

  9. Consider a particle undergoing a one-dimensional random walk, taking steps of length \(a\) to the right or left with equal probability. What is the probability distribution of the particle's position after \(n\) steps?

  10. Consider a particle undergoing an \(n\)-dimensional random walk, taking steps in each direction with equal probability. What is the probability that the particle will come back to the origin?

  11. Calculate the repulsion force the light rays from the Sun apply onto the Earth.

  12. Describe the properties of a material that makes up a liquid planet, so that however much mass is removed, the radius of the planet stays the same.

  13. Find the oscillation period of a spherical ball oscillating in water.\(^1\)

  14. Two small, identical particles have opposing charges of magnitude \(q\). They are separated by a distance \(L\) and left free. Find the time it takes them to collide as a function of the separation distance.\(^3\)
